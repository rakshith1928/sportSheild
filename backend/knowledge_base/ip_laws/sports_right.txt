Sports Broadcasting and Media Rights

International Framework:
1. Sports organizations own broadcast rights to their events
2. Media rights are sold exclusively to broadcasters
3. Unauthorized redistribution violates multiple laws
4. Digital piracy of sports content is a global problem

Types of Protected Sports Media:
1. Live match broadcasts
2. Highlight packages
3. Official team logos and badges
4. Player images and likeness
5. Official match photography
6. Team uniforms and merchandise designs
7. Trophy and award imagery
8. Stadium and venue footage

Common Violations:
1. Unauthorized streaming on social media
2. Reposting official match highlights without license
3. Using team logos without permission
4. Selling merchandise with protected designs
5. Screen recording and redistribution of broadcasts

How To File A Takedown Notice:
1. YouTube: youtube.com/copyright_complaint_form
2. Facebook/Instagram: facebook.com/help/contact/1758255661104383
3. Twitter/X: help.twitter.com/forms/dmca
4. General DMCA: send to platform's designated DMCA agent

Recommended Actions By Severity:
Low (similarity 0.75-0.85):
- Monitor the URL
- Send informal cease and desist

Medium (similarity 0.85-0.92):
- File platform takedown notice
- Send formal cease and desist letter

High (similarity above 0.92):
- File DMCA takedown immediately
- Consider legal action
- Report to sports organization legal team
