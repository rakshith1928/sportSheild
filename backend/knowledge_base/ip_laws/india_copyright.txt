Indian Copyright Act 1957

Section 13 - Works Protected:
Copyright shall subsist in:
1. Original literary, dramatic, musical and artistic works
2. Cinematograph films
3. Sound recordings
Sports broadcasts and sports media fall under cinematograph films and artistic works.

Section 51 - Infringement of Copyright:
Copyright is infringed when any person without license:
1. Does anything the exclusive right of which is conferred on the owner
2. Permits for profit any place to be used for communication of infringing work
3. Sells, distributes, or imports infringing copies

Section 55 - Civil Remedies:
- Injunction to stop infringement
- Damages or account of profits
- Delivery of infringing copies

Section 63 - Criminal Penalties:
- Minimum 6 months imprisonment
- Maximum 3 years imprisonment
- Fine between Rs 50,000 and Rs 2,00,000
- Repeat offenders face higher penalties

Sports Broadcasting Rights in India:
- BCCI holds exclusive rights to cricket broadcasts
- IPL media rights are strictly protected
- Unauthorized streaming of IPL matches is illegal
- Social media clips of matches require official license
- Sports organizations can file FIR under Section 63

Digital Rights:
- Section 65A protects technological protection measures
- Circumventing DRM is a criminal offense
- Online piracy of sports content is prosecutable
